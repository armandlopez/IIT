    Project Idea

    Overview

    “We Can Communicate” is an app that will be created that is available to download on IOS/Android in which its main function is allow for communication between non-deaf people and people who are deaf and vice versa. This app will allow people with no knowledge in sign language to easily to communicate with those who have impaired hearing allowing us to “build bridges, not walls” (Suzy Kassem) between both parties. The app will primarily be focused in Australia using Australian Sign Language (AUSLAN) but could be upgraded to include other sign languages and also other spoken languages other than English.

    Motivation

    Communication between people is very important and there are many apps that is used to translate one language to another but there is a very limited number of apps that are reliable enough to convert sign language to spoken language and vice versa. This project will be popular on the market due to its limited competition and also acts to help improve the society. Currently in Australia, the amount deaf people using Australian Sign Language (AUSLAN) is 82%. With the overwhelming majority of deaf people using sign language, communicating with those who don’t know sign language is a big problem.

    Description

    The product will be an app that will be available for download on both IOS and Android devices. The app’s interface will consist of the top 75% of the screen displaying what is being captured on the camera and the other 25% of the bottom of the screen will consists of buttons that bring up menus and settings. For three of the clickable buttons, one will be used to switch which camera is being used, the other will be used to determine if speech is being converted to sign language or sign language to speech, and the last button will be the record button which will used to record the speech or sign language and turn into its respective translation. For speech to sign language, after a phrase has been recorded, it is followed by a fully body animation performing the translated speech into sign language. When translating sign language to speech, the user will record themselves doing sign language and afterwards the app will show a text translation of the action. The app will be available in Australia as the primary sign language that will be used is AUSLAN (Australia Sign Language). AUSLAN is conveyed using hand, arm, and body movements hence why the animation will include a fully body to display those movements. Due to AUSLAN using two hands for the language rather than ASL (American Sign Language) which uses one hand, the app was designed with the option to use the outward camera to allow the other person to record the person performing the sign language. AUSLAN has a total of 68 hand movements and in order for the app to assign what word is associated with the action, a database will be created with the predefined meanings for each action. The app will receive the speech or action input, in which a built-in program in the app will assign it to its matching translation in the database. The program will then take the translation and output it into either an animation or text form based on the input. Due to the apps availability being only in Australia, it prevents the users from using other sign languages and spoken languages other than English, creating a minor barrier for those who can’t speak English and or people who use ASL to communicate.

    Tools and Technologies

    To create the app, the equipment that will be primarily used is a laptop/desktop computer in order to design the code create the database for the app. Motion cameras will also be needed to record the predefined actions that will be present in the app. To allow the app to perform these functions voice recognition software needs to be used. An open-source voice recognition software such a KALDI will be used identify speech and a licenced motion recognition software motion will be used to record the movements and will be implemented in a database. A database software that is able to store movement as information will be required in order for it to assign it with its translation for speech.

    Skills Required

    In order to create the app, the skills needed is a team who are very experienced in the required fields. An individual must be knowledgeable in programming, with experience in a wide variety of coding languages and complexities as a software such as KALDI uses c++. As such, installation of necessary programs will be based on what coding language will be primarily used. There also needs to be a person who is knowledgeable in motion recognition, knowing what type of motion cameras are required and where they need to be installed to reduce the amount of error that is captured.

    Outcome

    If the project is successful, people who are hearing impaired do not have to worry about being unable to communicate with those who can hear and vice versa because the app will remove that communication barrier. Although not everyone will have the app, should a situation occur where the app is needed, the app can be quickly downloaded from the app store and communication can be established. Eventually the funds generated from the app can be used to improve the app and branch out to other countries. This app is another way to help those who have disabilities to live out their daily lives with more ease.
